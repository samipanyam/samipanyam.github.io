First Reading

June 30, 2023

The first book I will start to read is an "INTRODUCTION TO ENVIRONMENTAL ENGINEERING" by Mackenzie L. Davis and David A. Cornwell. I think that this will be a good place to start and learn about the basics of environmental engineering. This is my post for the first 2 to 3 parts of the 1st chapter.

In the first section, the book talks about what exactly environmental engineering is. I didn't realize that there is a lot more to environmental engineering than controlling pollution in the soil and water. It also deals with the areas of sanitation and water supplies. I learned that it deals with engineering pronblems that come from public health. THis includes arthropod borne diseases. I am now very curious on what those diseases are.

In the second and third sections, I learned about the rules and codes that an environmental engineer have to follow. These were all very straightforward as they talked about how a environmental engineer must be truthful to the public and use their skills wisely and correctly. However, It is very interesting the idea that some of these rules come into a gray area when issues are more complicated. For example, I think it is interesting how if the EPA puts a law into place about disinfecting water even if that would kill beneficial microorganisms, you have to make a decision on what to do and how to do it. With my limited knowledge, I would probably follow the EPA's decision, but I think it has to be in the judgement of the engineer.
